Chapter 11

    1. Briefly describe the four gates explained in Chapter 11. Describe the behavior of each gate based on the input values to the gate.
  • AND gate is a gate that has 2 inputs and one out put. A light bulb (output) is only lit when both relays (inputs) are triggered. If either input is turned on (but not both) the lightbulb(output) turns off.
  • OR gate is a gate that has 2 inputs and one output. A light bulb (output) is only lit when one or the other relays (inputs) are triggered. Or if both inputs are triggered.
  • NOR (opposite of OR) gate is a gate that has 2 inputs and one output. When both of the inputs are off, the lightbulb (output) turns on. If both inputs are turned on the lightbulb (output) turns off.
  • NAND (opposite of AND) gate is a gate that has 2 inputs and one output. It is a gate when both inputs are on the lightbulb (output) turns off. When one or the other inputs is turned on (but not both) the lightbulb(output) turns on.



Chapter 12

    2. A half adder is built from how many "sub components" and has how many inputs and outputs?
  • Sub components: (2) XOR & AND
  • Inputs: 2
  • Outputs: 1
    3. A full adder is built from how many "sub components" and has how many inputs and outputs?
  • Sub components: (3) 2 half adders, 1 OR gate
  • Inputs: 4
  • Outputs: 2
    4. How many total inputs and outputs are there for an 8-bit adding circuit?
  • We need 8 full adders for our 8-bit adding machine.
  • Inputs: 32
  • Outputs: 16
