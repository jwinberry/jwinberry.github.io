Chapter 4
1. What do “front ends” and “back ends” mean to software developers? a) You the user are the “front end.” The front end is the the part of the program that deals with you. The back end is where the results of the front end events and inputs go so that the computer can make sense of them, save them, and retrieve them.
2. What did the Lego Hypothesis refer to? a) In the future all programs will be built out of reusable parts. Software parts will be available worldwide. Software engineering with be set free from the mundane necessity of programming.
3. Give one reason why the Lego Hypothesis seems to not work so well. a) If projects were like like the Lego hypothesis the would be small, invisible, and substitutable. They would be more similar to one another.
